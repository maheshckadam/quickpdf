10 PDF tricks every office worker should know

March 18, 2026 · 7 min read

Most people use PDFs daily and have no idea what they can actually do with them. Here are 10 tricks that take minutes to learn and save hours forever.

1. Extract a single page from a giant PDF

You have a 200-page contract. You only need page 47. Don't email the whole thing.

Open Split PDF, drop in the file, click on page 47's thumbnail, hit "Extract." Done — you have a one-page PDF.

2. Combine multiple invoices into one file

Your accountant asked for "all your invoices for Q4." Don't send 47 separate emails. Open Merge PDF, drop them all in, drag to sort by date, hit Merge. One clean file.

3. Reduce a too-big-to-email PDF

Email size limits are typically 25 MB. Your scanned document is 78 MB. Run it through Compress PDF at medium quality. Usually 70-90% smaller, with quality loss small enough that nobody will notice.

4. Make a scanned PDF searchable

Old PDFs are often just images of pages — you can't search them, can't copy text, can't even highlight. Run them through OCR PDF. Now you can Ctrl+F your way through them.

5. Sign a contract without printing

Don't print, sign, scan, email, then recycle the paper. Drop the contract into Sign PDF, draw your signature with your trackpad, place it on the right page. Send. Done in under a minute.

6. Add page numbers to a deck

Investor decks, reports, manuals — almost everything benefits from page numbers, and most people forget. Open Edit PDF, choose "Page numbers," pick your format ("Page 1 of 20" is professional), apply. Looks instantly more polished.

7. Watermark drafts to prevent confusion

If you send 5 versions of a draft contract over 3 weeks, the recipient won't remember which is the latest. Watermark each with "DRAFT v3 — Mar 18" before sending. Watermark PDF takes 10 seconds.

8. Convert a PDF to images for embedding

You want to put a chart from a PDF into a slide deck. Don't screenshot it (low quality). Use PDF to JPG at high quality, get a clean PNG, embed that. Sharp on every projector.

9. Reorganize pages without re-creating the document

Your designer sent you a 30-page brochure with the cover at the end and the back cover at the start. Don't ask them to redo it. Open Organize PDF, drag pages around to fix the order, save. Two minutes.

10. Get the gist of a long document in seconds

Someone forwarded you a 40-page report and asked "thoughts?" You don't have 90 minutes. Run it through our AI Summarizer, get a 120-word summary, decide if it's worth a full read. (For most reports: it isn't, and the summary is enough to reply intelligently.)

Bonus: keyboard tricks in your PDF reader

Once the file is in front of you, your reader has shortcuts most people don't use:

  • Ctrl/Cmd + F — search the document (works on any selectable-text PDF)
  • Ctrl/Cmd + Shift + 6 — rotate the page (in Preview on Mac)
  • Page Down / Spacebar — next page (Spacebar is universal)
  • Ctrl/Cmd + 0 — fit to window (in most readers)
  • Ctrl/Cmd + L — fullscreen presentation mode

The bigger lesson

PDF tools used to require Adobe Acrobat, which costs hundreds of dollars a year. Today you can do all of the above for free, in a browser, without uploading your files anywhere. The friction that existed for decades is gone — but only if you know the tools exist.
